Where is the Pyramid of Giza?

The Pyramid of Giza, also known as the Great Pyramid, is located on the Giza Plateau near Cairo, Egypt. It is one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World and was built during the Fourth Dynasty of the Old Kingdom of Ancient Egypt, around 2580–2560 BCE, for Pharaoh Khufu. The pyramid is a marvel of ancient engineering, constructed with massive limestone and granite blocks. It originally stood at 146.6 meters (481 feet) but is slightly shorter today due to the loss of the outer casing stones. The Pyramid of Giza is significant for its historical and cultural importance, representing the architectural and technological prowess of the ancient Egyptians. Its construction has sparked numerous theories and debates about the methods used and the purpose behind its design, adding to its mystique and allure. Additionally, the Great Pyramid is believed to have served as a tomb for Pharaoh Khufu, with intricate passageways and chambers inside.
