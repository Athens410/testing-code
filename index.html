About

"How to Get Away with Murder" is a legal drama and mystery television series that revolves around the life of Annalise Keating, a brilliant and charismatic law professor and criminal defence attorney. The show is known for its complex and suspenseful narrative, with several intertwined storylines. Here's a general overview of what the series is about: Annalise Keating (Viola Davis) :
Annalise is a formidable defence attorney and a professor at Middleton University. She selects a group of her top law students to work for her at her law firm. Annalise is a complex character who often finds herself entangled in morally ambiguous situations. The Keating Five: The main narrative of the series revolves around a group of five law students who are handpicked by Annalise to work for her firm. These students become deeply involved in high-stakes, real-life legal cases and are forced to confront their own moral dilemmas. Murder Mysteries : The series is framed around a series of murder mysteries. In the very first episode, a murder takes place, and the series unfolds as a series of flashbacks, flash-forwards, and present-day events as viewers are kept in suspense regarding the identity of the victim and the murderer. Character Development: The show delves into the lives of the main characters, exploring their personal and professional struggles, secrets, and relationships. Legal Cases: Each season features a different legal case that Annalise and her students are tasked with solving or defending. These cases often intersect with the main murder mystery storyline. The series is known for its intricate plot twists, morally complex characters, and its exploration of themes such as justice, ethics, and the consequences of one's actions. It received critical acclaim and has a dedicated fan following. If you enjoy legal dramas with elements of mystery and suspense, "How to Get Away with Murder" is a series worth checking out!
